New Climate Legislation Passes with Bipartisan Support

Landmark bill sets ambitious targets for carbon reduction and creates millions of new clean energy jobs across America.

The Climate Action and Clean Energy Jobs Act of 2028 passed both houses of Congress today with surprising bipartisan support, marking a significant shift in the political landscape around climate policy. The comprehensive legislation sets a target of 100% clean electricity by 2035 and net-zero emissions economy-wide by 2045.

Key provisions include:

  • $3 trillion investment in clean energy infrastructure over 10 years
  • Creation of an estimated 10 million new jobs in renewable energy sectors
  • Establishment of a carbon pricing mechanism with revenue directed to affected communities
  • Significant funding for climate resilience projects in vulnerable areas

"This is a historic moment for climate action in America," said Dr. Emma Rodriguez, climate policy expert at the National Climate Institute. "After years of gridlock, we're finally seeing the kind of bold action that science demands."
